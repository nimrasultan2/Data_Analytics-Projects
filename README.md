# The Campus Cravings Report: Student Eating Habit Dashboard
### Visualizing the Intersection of Academic Life & Nutrition

---

## Project Overview
This dashboard explores the dietary choices of 125 university students, uncovering how the stress of campus life impacts nutrition. It moves beyond simple calorie counting to look at **behavioral drivers** like boredom, exercise frequency, and gender-based dining trends.

> **The Vibe:** Academic aesthetic meets behavioral data. Minimalist lavender theme.

---

## Data Storytelling: Key Insights
* **The Boredom-Food Connection:** Nearly **40% of students** (49/125) admit to eating comfort food out of boredom, highlighting a direct link between emotional state and diet.
* **The "Ideal vs. Reality" Gap:** While "Changing eating habits" is the #1 ideal goal (44 students), the donut chart shows that **48% still follow an unhealthy diet.**
* **Dining Out Dynamics:** A fascinating gender split shows that **58% of those who eat out "Every Meal" are Female**, while **75% of those eating out 3-5 times a week are Male.**
* **Veggie Velocity:** On a positive note, a significant chunk of the student body (53 students) is eating veggies "Every Day," showing a strong sub-culture of health-conscious learners.

---

## Strategic Roadmap & Recommendations
1. **Stress-Eating Support:** University administrations should implement de-stressing events during exam seasons to provide healthier outlets for the 40% of students eating out of boredom.
2. **Dining Hall Optimization:** Since 42 students prefer "Ethnic Food," campus dining should diversify their menus to match these cultural cravings and reduce off-campus spending.
3. **Gender-Targeted Campaigns:** Nutrition workshops could be tailored—focusing on "Healthy Dining Out" choices for male students, who represent the majority of frequent diners.
4. **Nutrition Conscious "Nudges":** With only 16 students identifying as "Nutrition Conscious," there is a massive opportunity for campus-wide "Study Smarter, Eat Better" awareness campaigns.

* **Tool:** Microsoft PowerBI, PowerQuery
